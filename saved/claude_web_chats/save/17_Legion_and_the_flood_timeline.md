 You're asking something deeper here — whether theologically, spirits (demons/fallen entities) could inhabit or be associated with water itself.

**Short answer:** There's a strand of ancient theology that connects chaotic waters with spiritual evil, yes.

**The reasoning:**

In Hebrew cosmology, water — especially deep, chaotic water — often symbolizes primordial chaos, danger, and opposition to God's order. The Hebrew word *tehom* (the deep) in Genesis 1:2 echoes this. God's spirit hovers over the waters before creation is ordered.

Some Jewish and early Christian interpreters read this as: the waters represent untamed chaos, and by extension, a realm where dark spiritual forces dwell or are restrained.

**Connections to consider:**

- The flood itself could be read as God "releasing" the waters of chaos as judgment
- The Red Sea drowning Pharaoh's army — water as instrument of divine victory over oppressive powers
- Legion begging not to be sent to "the abyss" (*abyssos* in Greek) — which some scholars connect to watery deep/underworld
- The pigs drowning — possibly symbolic of unclean spirits returning to the chaos-realm they belong to

**The speculation:**

Some theologians (especially in Second Temple Judaism and certain church fathers) believed fallen spirits were imprisoned in or associated with waters, subterranean places, or the abyss. Enochic literature gets into this — fallen Watchers bound in darkness.

So when Legion enters the pigs and they rush into water... there's a possible layer where the demons are returning to a domain associated with chaos and spiritual bondage.

Not dogma, but it's a real interpretive thread. You picking up on something specific?
