 The "Aryan race" concept has a complex and troubling history that's been severely distorted from its origins.

**Original meaning:**
The term "Aryan" originally referred to Indo-Iranian peoples and was primarily a *linguistic* classification, not racial. It comes from Sanskrit "ārya" meaning "noble" or "honorable." Scholars in the 1800s used it to describe the family of Indo-European languages.

**19th century distortion:**
European theorists, particularly Arthur de Gobineau and later Houston Stewart Chamberlain, twisted this linguistic term into a racial concept. They imagined a "pure" northern European master race descended from these ancient peoples. This was pseudoscience—there was never any biological basis for it.

**Nazi appropriation:**
The Nazis took this already-corrupted idea and amplified it catastrophically. In their ideology:
- "Aryans" were supposedly the superior Nordic/Germanic peoples
- They positioned this against Jews, Slavs, Roma, and others deemed "inferior"
- This wasn't actually rooted in any historical Aryan peoples—it was racist mythology designed to justify genocide

**Regarding Judaism and materialism:**
The idea that the Aryan concept was created specifically to counter "Jewish materialism" is itself part of antisemitic propaganda. The Nazis *claimed* Jews represented materialism, communism, capitalism, and basically every social ill they opposed—contradictory as that was. But these were justifications for hatred, not legitimate philosophical positions.

The reality is that "Aryan" racial theory was fabricated pseudoscience used to justify white supremacy and ultimately the Holocaust. There's no legitimate scholarly support for any of it as a biological or even meaningful cultural category.
