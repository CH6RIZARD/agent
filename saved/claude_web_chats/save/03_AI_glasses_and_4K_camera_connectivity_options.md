**RED FLAG** - here's why:

**AP Mode Only = Limited**
- Means glasses create their own WiFi hotspot
- Your phone connects TO the glasses
- Only good for transferring files after you record
- No real-time streaming to cloud/social
- No live preview on your phone while recording
- Can't use your phone's internet while connected

**What you actually want:**
- **Client mode** - glasses connect to YOUR phone's hotspot or home WiFi
- Lets you stream live
- Real-time backup to cloud
- Use phone normally while glasses sync in background

**Why cheap glasses only do AP mode:**
- Easier/cheaper to implement
- Less power drain on glasses
- Hides that the chip can't handle real streaming

**Questions to ask before buying:**
- Can it connect to my phone's hotspot? (client mode)
- Can I live stream while recording?
- Does the app work while I'm on cellular?

If they only mention "WiFi transfer" or "download via app" - that's AP mode only. If they say "live streaming" or "real-time sync" - that's the good stuff.

## ASSISTANT

 **Most AI glasses do NOT have noise canceling** - here's the breakdown:

---

**What they DO have:**

**ENC (Environmental Noise Cancellation)**
- Only works on YOUR voice during calls
- Filters background noise so caller hears you clearly
- Does NOT cancel noise for YOUR ears

**Dual mics**
- Helps with wind/ambient noise in recordings
- Better voice pickup for AI commands
- Again - for recording quality, not your listening

---

**What they DON'T have:**

**ANC (Active Noise Cancellation)**
- The kind that blocks outside sound for YOU
- Like AirPods Pro or Sony headphones
- Would need sealed earbuds, not open speakers

---

**Why no ANC on glasses:**

- Open-ear speakers by design (safety, awareness)
- No seal = can't cancel noise
- You're supposed to hear surroundings
- Battery drain would be massive

---

**If you want noise canceling + smart features:**

| Option | Trade-off |
|--------|-----------|
| AI glasses + ANC earbuds | Two devices, looks weird |
| Bose/Sony audio glasses | Better sound, no camera/AI |
| Just wait | Future models might figure it out |

---

**Bottom line:**

AI glasses = you hear everything around you

That's a feature for safety but a con if you want immersive audio.
