 Psilocybin is the main psychoactive compound in magic mushrooms. Here's how it works:

**Metabolism**

When you ingest psilocybin, your body converts it into **psilocin** through a process called dephosphorylation (your liver removes a phosphate group). Psilocin is the compound that actually produces the psychoactive effects.

**Mechanism in the brain**

Psilocin primarily binds to **serotonin receptors**, especially the 5-HT2A receptor. This binding triggers a cascade of effects:

1. **Increased neural connectivity** — Brain regions that don't normally communicate start talking to each other. The default mode network (associated with ego and self-referential thinking) becomes less active while other networks become more interconnected.

2. **Altered perception** — Visual cortex activity changes, producing the characteristic visual distortions, colors, and patterns.

3. **Emotional shifts** — Limbic system activation can produce intense emotions, ranging from euphoria to anxiety.

**Timeline**

Effects typically begin 20-40 minutes after ingestion, peak around 2-3 hours, and last 4-6 hours total. The body clears psilocin relatively quickly.

**Research context**

There's growing clinical research on psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression, PTSD, and end-of-life anxiety. The FDA has granted it "breakthrough therapy" designation for depression, meaning trials are ongoing but it's not yet approved.

### **The Skin Absorption Challenge**

**Current Status:** Psilocybin/psilocin are **poorly suited** for transdermal delivery naturally.

**Why:**
- **Psilocybin** = hydrophilic (water-loving), large molecule with phosphate group
- **Psilocin** = better but still challenges
- Skin's stratum corneum blocks most large, polar molecules

## Advantages of Transdermal Route

1. **Bypasses first-pass metabolism** — more bioavailable
2. **Sustained release** — steadier levels, longer duration  
3. **Reduced GI side effects** — no nausea
4. **Controlled dosing** — can remove patch to stop
5. **Discrete application** — less obvious than eating mushrooms
6. **Precise dosing** — measured application vs. variable mushroom potency

## Disadvantages

1. **Slower onset** — emergency situations problematic
2. **Skin irritation** — DMSO can burn, cause rashes
3. **Variable absorption** — skin thickness, hydration varies
4. **Higher dose needed** — skin barrier reduces efficiency
5. **Preparation complexity** — requires pharmaceutical formulation
6. **Unknown safety** — no clinical trials on transdermal psychedelics
